Payment Friction and Spending: Double ML Evidence

A causal ML project on payment friction and daily spending in diary data.

Co-authored with Jerrold Huang, Jiaxun Gao, Xiangyu Zhang, and Mingyu Quan.

GitHubReport PDF

Short version

What it was

Are lower-friction payment methods associated with higher daily spending after controlling for demographics, timing, and basket composition?

Data I touched

The 2024 Atlanta Fed Diary of Consumer Payment Choice, organized as 5,583 individuals and 22,332 in-person person-days.

My part

I helped define the person-day treatment and outcome, estimate the nuisance models, and turn the heterogeneity results into spending profiles.

Where it landed

A reproducible notebook pipeline, a written report, respondent-level diagnostics, and CATE plots by ticket size, income, and education.
