Payment Friction and Spending: Double ML Evidence

A causal ML project on whether easier payment methods are associated with higher daily spending.

Co-authored with Jerrold Huang, Jiaxun Gao, Xiangyu Zhang, and Mingyu Quan.

GitHubReport PDF

Short version

What it was

Do credit, debit, mobile, ACH, and OBBP payments raise daily spending after residualizing demographics, timing, and basket composition?

Data I touched

The 2024 Atlanta Fed Diary of Consumer Payment Choice, organized as 5,583 individuals and 22,332 in-person person-days.

My part

I helped design the causal ML workflow, define the person-day treatment and outcome, estimate nuisance models, and translate heterogeneity results into interpretable spending patterns.

Where it landed

A reproducible notebook pipeline, a written report, respondent-level diagnostics, and CATE plots by ticket size, income, and education.
