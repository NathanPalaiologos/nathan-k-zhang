Illumi-Naughty: Nighttime Luminosity and Crime in Vancouver

A Vancouver panel-data project linking satellite nighttime light with neighborhood crime.

Co-authored with Irene Berezin, Jerrold Huang, Jaehyung Park, and William Clinton Co.

ECON 398 repoReport PDFExtension repo

Short version

What it was

Can satellite-measured nighttime light help explain variation in Vancouver crime after neighborhood and time controls?

Data I touched

NASA Black Marble nighttime luminosity, Vancouver Police Department crime records, census-tract geography, and economic/weather covariates.

My part

I built the geospatial panel, produced the maps and scatter diagnostics for the ECON 398 project, and later extended the design in NTL-crime work.

Where it landed

A paper, notebooks, Vancouver neighborhood maps, luminosity and crime choropleths, and panel data for regression.
