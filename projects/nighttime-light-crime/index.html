Illumi-Naughty: Nighttime Luminosity and Crime in Vancouver

A Vancouver panel-data project with satellite nighttime light, neighborhood crime, and a title that got away from us.

Co-authored with Irene Berezin, Jerrold Huang, Jaehyung Park, and William Clinton Co.

ECON 398 repoReport PDFExtension repo

Short version

What it was

Can satellite-measured nighttime light help explain variation in Vancouver crime after controlling for neighborhood and time patterns?

Data I touched

NASA Black Marble nighttime luminosity, Vancouver Police Department crime records, census-tract geography, and economic/weather covariates.

My part

I contributed to the ECON 398 project by building the geospatial panel, producing diagnostic maps and scatter plots, and extending the design into later NTL-crime work.

Where it landed

A paper, notebooks, Vancouver neighborhood maps, luminosity/crime choropleths, and regression-ready panel data.
