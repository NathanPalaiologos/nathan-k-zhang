Technology Exposure and Labor-Market Data Pipeline

Patent text, occupational tasks, and panel data on workers, cleaned and checked before any measure enters a regression.

Built with Dr. J. Frank Li and Dr. Cheng Chen at UBC Sauder.

Short version

What it was

How can patent text and occupational task data become exposure measures for worker-level empirical research?

Data I touched

Patent text and metadata, O*NET occupational task measures, individual employment histories, and occupation crosswalks.

My part

I worked on the parquet-first pipeline, occupation-link audits, patent-task similarity jobs, and the variables that carry employment-sorting ideas into the panel.

Where it landed

Worker-level panels, exposure tables, sample funnels, occupation rankings, and replication notes.
