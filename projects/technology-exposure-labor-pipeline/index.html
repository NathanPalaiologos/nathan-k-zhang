Technology Exposure and Labor-Market Data Pipeline

Patent text, occupation tasks, worker panels, and a lot of checks before anything becomes a regression variable.

Built with Prof. Frank Li and Prof. Cheng Chen at UBC Sauder.

Short version

What it was

How can I turn patent text and occupation task data into exposure measures that are useful for worker-level empirical research?

Data I touched

Patent text and metadata, O*NET occupation measures, worker histories, and occupation crosswalks.

My part

I built parquet-first data pipelines, audited occupation links, ran patent-task similarity jobs, and turned employment-sorting ideas into measurable variables for regression work.

Where it landed

Regression-ready worker panels, exposure tables, sample funnels, interpretable occupation rankings, and replication-oriented documentation.
