Praxis UBC AI Literacy and Research Computing Modules

Teaching modules on LLMs, embeddings, CNNs, and research computing for students coming from economics and applied research.

Built with the UBC Economics COMET/Praxis team for the prAxIs TLEF project.

Praxis UBCGitHub

Short version

What it was

How can AI and ML methods be taught with enough intuition, code, and context for non-CS students to use them in research?

Data I touched

Public teaching datasets, course-specific examples, and open Praxis UBC materials.

My part

I wrote and maintained notebook-style materials, visual examples, code paths, and publishing workflows for the AI-literacy and research-computing modules.

Where it landed

Published Quarto/Jupyter modules, reusable teaching examples, visual explanations, and GitHub Actions-backed course materials.

Figures from the project

Modules I authored or co-authored

LLM Prediction Modules

I built materials that connect next-word prediction, token probabilities, and course-specific examples for students learning LLMs through economics and applied research.

I used small examples so students could see how token probabilities and news snippets become model inputs.

LLM prediction moduleCTree / CEA version

CNNs and Convolutions

I helped turn image filters, kernels, and CNN intuition into computational notebooks for teaching.

I kept the path from pixels to convolution to classification explicit for students without a CS background.

CNN moduleConvolutions module

Image and Text Embeddings

I contributed applied modules using image and text embeddings in humanities and social-science research settings.

I focused on making the steps visible: raw image or text, extracted features, model output, and the interpretation students should question.
