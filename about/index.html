A bit more context

I study statistics and economics at UBC, and I keep drifting toward projects where the model is only half the story. The other half is deciding what the data can support, writing down the assumptions, and keeping the analysis honest when the first result looks a little too convenient.

Outside research, I run around UBC, cycle through Vancouver, watch too many films and anime, play games sometimes, and let small curiosities become data projects after they refuse to stay small.

The problems I like

I like applied statistics when the hard part is not only fitting a model. The data have to be built, the assumptions have to show their face, and the explanation has to survive contact with the research question.

What I am loading next

My next-year course stack is basically me telling future me to stop bluffing and learn the theory properly: real analysis, statistical inference and asymptotic theory, stochastic processes, advanced machine learning, deep learning, NLP, and game theory.

My annoying research habit

I ask boring questions early. Where did this row come from? What dropped out of the sample? Is the impressive chart mostly a missingness problem wearing a nicer shirt? The checks are not glamorous, but they save time later.
