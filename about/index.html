About

A bit more context

I study statistics and economics at UBC, and most of my projects sit somewhere between data work and modeling. I care about the part before the estimate: how the dataset was built, what assumptions are being made, and whether the result still makes sense after basic checks.

Outside research I run around UBC, cycle in Vancouver, watch more films and anime than planned, and occasionally let a small curiosity turn into a data project.

The problems I like

I like applied statistics when the work depends on both the model and the dataset behind it. That usually means cleaning inputs, checking joins, writing down sample restrictions, and explaining the result without hiding the caveats.

What I am studying next

My next-year courses lean into the theory I want under my empirical work: real analysis, statistical inference and asymptotic theory, stochastic processes, advanced machine learning, deep learning, NLP, and game theory.

My research habit

I tend to ask data questions early. Where did this row come from? What fell out of the sample? Is the chart showing signal, or mostly missingness and selection? The checks are plain, but they save time later.
